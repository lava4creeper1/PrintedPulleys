## Printing Information
<p>
To minimise the effect of the print seam when the bearing is spinning, use a scarf seam in your slicer.<br>
I tested this with 0.2 mm layer height and printed in PLA+ on an ender 3 v3 KE.
</p>

## Construction Information
**You will need:** <p>8 4mm steel ball bearings.<p>
- To construct the bearing, balance seven of these in the track at the bottom of the outer shell, stood vertically.
- Place the inner shell on top of these seven in line with the outer shell, being careful not to dislodge any of the balls. 
- Lift the inner shell so it is pressed against the top of the outer shell. There should now be enough space to insert the eighth ball. You will have to push the balls already in out of the way to insert the final ball; be careful to not dislodge them from the track. This is the most finnicky part and may take multiple attempts.
- Use a toothpick or other small device to pull at least one ball to the top of the bearing. Once this has been done, the shells will be held in position relative to one another and the bearing can be laid flat.
- Place the bearing on top of the cage with the flat side facing down. Use the toothpick or small device to place one ball on top of each indent, so all eight are equally spaced around the bearing and push straight down. A reasonable amount of force may be required, but the cage should snap into place.
- The bearing may require some working to get it spinning freely. If it is not smooth enough after this, a small amount of oil or lubricant may help.
